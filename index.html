Which Country has the Most Innovation Potential?

Research & Development (R&D) expenditure is a convenient index to measure a country's innovation capacity.

Today, the US and China lead the world, both increasing their expenditure rapidly. But this is mainly due to the size and growth of the economy in both countries.

In terms of expenditure per GDP, these countries do not necessarily lead the world. There are other players that focus much more of their economy on R&D.

Then, who is actually focusing on R&D?

At the top of the per GDP expenditure, we have Israel and South Korea, both with heavy focus on emerging technologies and the tech industry (and military-related R&D spending, given the geopolitical situations the two countries are in).

Following are Taiwan, Sweden, and Japan, all with strong technology-related industries.

Finland, home to Nokia, has also had high percentage expenditures in recent years.

While China has been increasing its expenditure, other East Asian players spend a much larger percentage of their economy on R&D. Korea and Taiwan have kept pace or even exceeded the growth rate of China, the dominant player of the region today.

Research has shown that a national sense of threat by external hazards (both economic and military competition) leads to increased spending on R&D, and subsequently to a strong innovation sector. The countries above very much fit into this picture, with regional economic and military competition abound.
